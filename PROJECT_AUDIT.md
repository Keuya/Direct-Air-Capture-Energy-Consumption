# Project Audit — Direct Air Capture Energy Consumption

## What this repository appears to contain
This repository is positioned as a lightweight analysis of the energy consumption of different Direct Air Capture methods, with basic processing and visualisation.

## Current strengths
- Clear and current topic with strong climate-tech relevance.
- Practical angle: energy intensity is one of the biggest constraints in DAC deployment.
- Suitable starting point for an engineering + analytics portfolio project.

## What is likely missing or underdeveloped
- The scope appears narrow and still early-stage.
- The analysis likely stops at descriptive comparison and does not yet answer the harder commercial questions.
- It is not yet clearly linked to system-level energy questions such as power source, heat source, cost, emissions intensity, or deployment geography.

## How to improve the next version
1. Compare DAC pathways by **electricity demand, heat demand, and total energy per tonne of CO2 removed**.
2. Add a **cost layer**: electricity cost, heat cost, and implied operating cost by geography.
3. Add an **emissions layer**: DAC powered by coal-heavy grids vs renewable-heavy grids.
4. Test a **Kenya / East Africa lens**: where low-carbon power could make certain carbon-removal pathways more viable.
5. Include **learning-curve assumptions** and future cost decline scenarios.

## Best way to align this project with your background
Reframe the repo from a general climate-tech script into an **energy systems decision model**.
That would connect better with your profile in renewable energy, strategy, and finance.

## Energy-sector problems this project can speak to
- High energy intensity of carbon-removal technologies
- Need for low-cost clean electricity and heat
- Competition for renewable power between industry, grids, and new climate technologies
- Technology selection under energy-constrained systems
